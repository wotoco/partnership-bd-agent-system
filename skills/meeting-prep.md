# meeting-prep — pre-call research brief

Before a call or meeting, research the person, their company, and recent context, then hand you a one-page brief so you walk in sharp.

**Give it:** a name + company (or a LinkedIn/company URL) and what the meeting is about.

---

## Research steps
1. **Person** — role, tenure, background, anything public they've said or posted recently
2. **Company** — what they do, size/stage, business model, recent news (**last 3 months**, with URLs)
3. **Context** — funding, launches, hires, pivots, partnerships relevant to your meeting goal
4. **Common ground** — shared connections, overlapping interests, mutual angles

Use a browser tool for login-gated pages, a scraper for public ones. Every fact gets a source.

---

## Output — the brief

```
# Meeting Brief — [Name], [Company]
Meeting: [goal] · [date]

## Who they are
[2–3 lines: role, background, what they care about]

## Company snapshot
[what they do · stage · 1 recent development w/ source]

## Why this matters to them
[their likely priority / pain right now]

## Your angle
[the value exchange — what you bring, what they get, where it overlaps]

## 3 questions to ask
1. … 2. … 3. …

## Watch-outs
[anything sensitive / red flags / things to avoid]
```

## Write-back (closes the loop)
After the meeting, append a row to `memory/outcomes.md` (Meeting table): what happened, their reaction, the follow-up, and one note for next time. Each prep then starts from accumulated account knowledge instead of a cold search — the brief gets deeper every cycle.

## Done when
- [ ] Every claim has a source, recent context is < 3 months
- [ ] The angle is a value exchange, not a generic intro
- [ ] 3 specific questions, not filler
- [ ] Post-meeting outcome logged to `memory/outcomes.md`
